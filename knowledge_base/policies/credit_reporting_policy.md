# Credit Reporting Complaint Guidance

Credit reporting complaints often involve incorrect account information, identity theft, disputes not investigated, outdated information, or accounts that do not belong to the consumer. These complaints are sensitive because they can affect credit access, loan pricing, employment screening, and customer trust.

The analyst should verify the complaint category, review supporting documentation, confirm whether the disputed information was reported by the institution, and route the case to the credit bureau dispute or furnishing operations team when applicable.

Customer responses should explain that the information will be reviewed, that supporting documentation may be required, and that the company will communicate the outcome after investigation. Do not promise removal or correction before verification.
